# Active Listening & Communication Styles

## Question 1  
### What are the steps / strategies to do Active Listening?

- Avoid getting distracted by your own thoughts  
- Always focus on the topic that the speaker is talking about  
- Try not to interrupt the other person  
- Let them finish speaking and then respond  
- Show that you are listening using body language (eye contact, nodding)  
- Take notes during important conversations  

---

## Question 2  
### According to Fisher’s model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?

Reflective listening means listening carefully and showing the other person that you truly understand them.

Key points are:
- Paying full attention to the speaker  
- Understanding both what they say and how they feel  
- Repeating or rephrasing their message in your own words  
- Not interrupting in the middle of the conversation  
- Asking clarifying questions if something is unclear  

---

## Question 3  
### What are the obstacles in your listening process?

Some obstacles in my listening process are:
- Getting distracted by my phone or surroundings  
- Thinking about my reply instead of listening  
- Making assumptions before the other person finishes  
- Lack of interest in the topic  

These reduce my ability to listen properly.

---

## Question 4  
### What can you do to improve your listening?

I can improve my listening by:
- Giving full attention and avoiding distractions  
- Maintaining eye contact  
- Letting the person finish speaking  
- Asking questions to clarify  

---

## Question 5  
### When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day-to-day life?

I switch to a passive communication style when:
- I want to avoid conflict  
- I talk to my superior or higher officials  
- I don’t want others to feel bad about the conversation with me  

---

## Question 6  
### When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?

I switch to an aggressive communication style when:
- I feel very angry or frustrated  
- Someone repeatedly ignores me  
- I feel disrespected  
- I am under pressure or stress  

---

## Question 7  
### When do you switch into Passive-Aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?

I switch to passive-aggressive communication when:
- I am upset but don’t want to say it directly  
- I feel hurt or ignored  

---

## Question 8  
### How can you make your communication assertive?

I can make my communication assertive by:
- Expressing my thoughts clearly and honestly  
- Using “I” statements (for example: *I feel*, *I think*)  
- Respecting others while standing up for myself  
- Controlling my emotions
